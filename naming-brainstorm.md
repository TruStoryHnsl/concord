# Concord Naming Schema — Brainstorm

## The Schema

**"Single-word descriptions of social contracts as described by a third party."**

These are nouns an outside observer would use to characterize the social arrangement happening between participants. Not what the participants call themselves — what someone watching would call the dynamic.

### Established Names
| Name | Feature | Social Contract |
|------|---------|-----------------|
| **Concord** | Main app | Mutual agreement. Harmony between parties. |
| **Servitude** | Headless server daemon | Obligated service. One party labors for others. |

## Sub-App Candidates by Feature Area

### Voice / Video / Calls

The social contract of synchronous conversation — agreeing to be present and heard.

| Word | Meaning | Fit | Notes |
|------|---------|-----|-------|
| **Parley** | A negotiation between opponents under truce | Strong | Pirates, diplomats, warring factions. Perfect for voice — you're agreeing to a temporary space of mutual listening. |
| **Counsel** | Advice given formally; a confidential consultation | Strong | Implies both intimacy and authority. Great for 1:1 calls. |
| **Colloquy** | A formal conversation or dialogue | Medium | Formal, perhaps too academic. But elegant. |
| **Audience** | A formal hearing granted by a person in authority | Strong | Beautiful inversion — the "call" is an audience you've granted. Your attention is the privilege. |
| **Convocation** | A formal gathering called by authority | Medium | More suited to group calls / conferencing. |
| **Oration** | A formal public speech | Weak | One-directional. Doesn't capture the back-and-forth. |
| **Discourse** | Formal discussion; exchange of ideas | Medium | Overlaps with forum territory. |
| **Parlance** | A way of speaking; the language of a group | Weak | More about language than the act of speaking. |
| **Testimony** | A formal statement of truth | Medium | Too judicial. But interesting for "bearing witness" in calls. |

**Top picks**: **Parley** (group calls), **Counsel** (1:1 calls), **Audience** (receiving a call)

### Forums / Public Discussion

| Word | Meaning | Fit | Notes |
|------|---------|-----|-------|
| **Edict** | A proclamation with force of law | Strong | Posts spread outward like a decree from a node. Mesh-scoped broadcasting IS a form of proclamation. |
| **Dispatch** | A message sent with speed and purpose | Strong | Perfect for hop-limited local forum posts — a dispatch from the field. |
| **Tidings** | News, information brought from elsewhere | Strong | Beautiful for gossip-protocol-delivered posts. "Tidings from 3 hops away." |
| **Gazette** | An official journal or newspaper | Medium | Implies curation. Might fit a curated/pinned-posts feature. |
| **Tribunal** | A court of judgment | Medium | Better for moderation. |
| **Assembly** | A gathering for deliberation | Strong | The act of people coming together to discuss. Fits the forum-as-place model. |
| **Manifesto** | A public declaration of intentions | Medium | Too political. But interesting for charter documents. |
| **Petition** | A formal request to an authority | Medium | Could fit a feature-request or proposal system. |
| **Proclamation** | A public announcement | Medium | Overlaps with Edict but less authoritative. |
| **Polemic** | A contentious argument | Weak | Too adversarial. |
| **Broadside** | A strongly worded critical publication | Weak | Aggressive. But historically apt (printed single-sheet proclamations). |
| **Bulletin** | An official statement or broadcast | Medium | Clean, functional. Less evocative. |

**Top picks**: **Tidings** (local/mesh forum), **Edict** (global announcements), **Assembly** (the forum space itself)

### Direct Messages / Private Conversation

The social contract of confidence — trusting someone with private communication.

| Word | Meaning | Fit | Notes |
|------|---------|-----|-------|
| **Confidence** | A secret shared in trust; the act of confiding | Excellent | "Speaking in confidence." The PERFECT word for encrypted DMs. The social contract IS mutual trust in secrecy. |
| **Compact** | A formal agreement between parties | Strong | DMs as a compact — both parties agree to the terms of private exchange. |
| **Pact** | A binding agreement, often secret | Strong | Darker than compact. Implies conspiratorial intimacy. |
| **Tryst** | A secret meeting between lovers | Medium | Too romantic/sexual. But the secrecy angle is right. |
| **Assignation** | A secret meeting; an appointment | Medium | Similar issues to tryst but more formal. |
| **Communion** | Intimate sharing; spiritual fellowship | Medium | Beautiful but religious overtones. |
| **Intimacy** | Close familiarity; private knowledge | Weak | Too abstract. |
| **Whisper** | Secret speech meant for one ear | Medium | Not really a "social contract" word. |
| **Covenant** | A solemn binding agreement | Strong | Heavy. Implies permanence. Good for long-standing DM relationships. |
| **Sacrament** | A sacred rite or bond | Medium | Too religious for general use. But the weight is right. |
| **Confessional** | A space for private disclosure | Medium | Implies one-way revelation. DMs are bidirectional. |

**Top picks**: **Confidence** (DMs as a feature), **Compact** (individual conversation), **Covenant** (persistent DM channels)

### Trust / Reputation / Attestation

The social contract of vouching — staking your reputation on another's character.

| Word | Meaning | Fit | Notes |
|------|---------|-----|-------|
| **Attestation** | A formal declaration that something is true | Strong | Already used in the codebase. Fits perfectly. |
| **Witness** | One who testifies to truth from personal knowledge | Strong | "I witness this node." The act of confirming reality. |
| **Surety** | A guarantee; a person who takes responsibility for another | Excellent | You ARE the surety for nodes you vouch for. Your reputation backs theirs. This is literally what the trust system does. |
| **Bondsman** | One who provides a bond/guarantee for another | Medium | Too gendered. But the concept is perfect. |
| **Indemnity** | Security against damage or loss | Medium | Too insurance-y. |
| **Warranty** | A guarantee of quality | Weak | Too commercial. |
| **Credence** | Belief in or acceptance of something as true | Strong | "Credence" as the reputation system. Beautiful — it IS about belief in a node's trustworthiness. |
| **Voucher** | One who vouches; a guarantee | Weak | Sounds like a coupon. |
| **Fealty** | A feudal oath of loyalty | Strong | Dark but evocative. "I pledge fealty" = I attest for this node. |
| **Allegiance** | Loyalty to a group, sovereign, or cause | Strong | Broader than fealty. Works for group-level trust. |

**Top picks**: **Surety** (trust/reputation system), **Credence** (trust score/level), **Witness** (the act of attesting)

### Governance / Voting / Proposals

The social contract of collective decision-making — submitting to the will of the group.

| Word | Meaning | Fit | Notes |
|------|---------|-----|-------|
| **Suffrage** | The right to vote | Excellent | This IS the governance system. The social contract of democratic participation. |
| **Franchise** | The right to vote; a granted privilege | Strong | "Franchise" as in the right to participate. Also implies something earned/granted. |
| **Mandate** | Authority given by the electorate; a command | Strong | The result of a vote IS a mandate. |
| **Ratification** | Formal approval of a decision | Medium | Too process-oriented. |
| **Referendum** | A direct vote on a specific issue | Strong | Each proposal IS a referendum. |
| **Plebiscite** | A vote by the entire electorate | Medium | Similar to referendum. More grandiose. |
| **Quorum** | The minimum needed for valid proceedings | Medium | A technical term but describes a social contract (we agree X people must participate). |
| **Sanction** | Official approval OR penalty (dual meaning) | Strong | Beautiful dual meaning — both the approval of a vote AND the consequence of breaking rules. |
| **Decree** | An authoritative order | Medium | Result of governance, not the process itself. |
| **Ordinance** | A municipal regulation | Weak | Too bureaucratic. |

**Top picks**: **Suffrage** (voting system), **Mandate** (proposal results), **Sanction** (enforcement)

|------|---------|-----|-------|
| **Dominion** | Sovereign authority over territory | Strong | Your view of the mesh map IS your dominion — the territory you can see and influence. |

| **Demesne** | Land attached to a manor; territory under direct control | Excellent | Obscure but perfect. Pronounced "deh-MAIN." Your demesne is the mesh visible from your perspective. The land you directly oversee. |

### Places / Servers / Organizations

The social contract of membership — belonging to and contributing to a collective.

| Word | Meaning | Fit | Notes |
|------|---------|-----|-------|
| **Fief** | Land granted in exchange for service | Excellent | A Place IS a fief — granted by the owner, maintained by members who contribute compute. The feudal metaphor is architecturally accurate. |
| **Stewardship** | Responsible management of something entrusted | Strong | Running a server/Place IS stewardship. You don't own the network — you steward a piece of it. |
| **Patronage** | Support/sponsorship of a person or cause | Strong | Place owners are patrons. Members receive the benefit. |
| **Fellowship** | Shared membership in a community | Strong | Clean, warm. A Place IS a fellowship. |
| **Congregation** | A gathering of people united by belief/purpose | Medium | Religious overtones but the meaning is right. |
| **Charter** | A founding document granting rights | Strong | Already used in the codebase (charter immutability). A Place's charter IS its founding contract. |
| **Enclave** | A small territory within a larger one | Strong | A private Place on the public mesh. |
| **Commune** | A group sharing resources and governance | Strong | Public governance Places are literally communes. |
| **Garrison** | A fortified place with troops stationed | Medium | Good for server-class nodes defending a Place. |
| **Manor** | An estate with tenants | Medium | Too feudal for collaborative spaces. |
| **Abbey** | A self-governing religious community | Medium | Self-governing community without the religious connotation... almost works. |
| **Lodge** | A meeting place for a fraternal organization | Strong | Private, member-only, rituals of entry. Very fitting. |

**Top picks**: **Fief** (a Place), **Stewardship** (hosting/admin role), **Lodge** (private Places), **Charter** (Place founding document)

### Tunnels / Encrypted Connections / VPN

The social contract of safe passage — guaranteeing transit through dangerous territory.

| Word | Meaning | Fit | Notes |
|------|---------|-----|-------|
| **Passage** | Safe transit; a narrow way through | Strong | A tunnel IS a passage through the hostile internet. |
| **Conduct** | Safe escort through dangerous territory (archaic) | Excellent | "Safe conduct" was a medieval document guaranteeing passage. A WireGuard tunnel IS a grant of safe conduct through the open internet. |
| **Escort** | Accompaniment for protection | Medium | Implies a person, not a path. |
| **Amnesty** | Protection from prosecution; safe harbor | Medium | Better for anonymous browsing. |
| **Transit** | The act of passing through | Weak | Too modern/mundane. |
| **Corridor** | A passage connecting places | Medium | Functional but not evocative. |
| **Conduit** | A channel for conveying something | Strong | Technical but fits — a conduit for encrypted traffic. |
| **Aqueduct** | A channel built to carry water | Weak | Too literal. |
| **Portcullis** | A fortified gate | Medium | More about access control than transit. |
| **Rampart** | A defensive wall | Weak | Static defense, not passage. |

**Top picks**: **Conduct** (tunnel system), **Passage** (individual tunnel connection), **Conduit** (the VPN layer)

| Word | Meaning | Fit | Notes |
|------|---------|-----|-------|
| **Amnesty** | Forgiveness; immunity from prosecution | Excellent | An anonymous node operates under amnesty — its past and identity are deliberately forgotten. The network agrees to not remember. |
| **Asylum** | Protection from persecution; a safe place | Strong | Seeking asylum from identification. |

### Moderation / Anti-Abuse

The social contract of justice — enforcing community norms through agreed-upon consequences.

| Word | Meaning | Fit | Notes |
|------|---------|-----|-------|
| **Tribunal** | A court empowered to judge | Excellent | Moderation IS a tribunal. The community convenes to judge behavior. |
| **Exile** | Banishment from one's community | Strong | Being banned from a Place. The ultimate social consequence. |
| **Censure** | Formal expression of disapproval | Strong | A warning or temporary sanction. Less severe than exile. |
| **Ostracism** | Social exclusion by group decision | Excellent | Historically, Athenian ostracism was a democratic vote to banish someone for 10 years. The vote-kick mechanic IS ostracism. |
| **Proscription** | An official ban; public condemnation | Strong | More formal than exile. Implies public listing of the banned. |
| **Inquisition** | A formal investigation, especially for heresy | Medium | Too loaded. But the investigative aspect fits moderation review. |
| **Interdict** | A prohibition or restraining order | Strong | A moderation action: "you are interdicted from this channel." |
| **Excommunication** | Expulsion from a community of faith | Medium | Religious overtones. But the social mechanic is identical to banning. |
| **Quarantine** | Enforced isolation | Medium | Could fit a "muted but not banned" state. |

**Top picks**: **Tribunal** (moderation system), **Ostracism** (democratic vote-ban), **Exile** (ban action), **Censure** (warning)

| **Conscription** | Compulsory enrollment for service | Strong | Nodes conscripted into compute duty. Dark but accurate for the mandatory aspect. |

| **Muster** | To assemble troops; to gather resources | Strong | Mustering compute resources for a task. |

### Encryption / Security Layer

The social contract of secrecy — all parties agree to protect shared information.

| Word | Meaning | Fit | Notes |
|------|---------|-----|-------|
| **Covenant** | A solemn, binding agreement | Excellent | E2E encryption IS a covenant — an unbreakable mutual promise that communication stays private. The cryptographic handshake IS the covenant ceremony. |
| **Seal** | An official mark guaranteeing authenticity | Strong | Encrypted messages are sealed. You break the seal to read them. |
| **Sacrament** | A sacred rite conveying grace | Medium | Too religious. But the weight matches the importance. |
| **Sanctum** | A sacred private place | Strong | Your encrypted storage IS your sanctum. |
| **Vault** | A secured storage chamber | Medium | Good for at-rest encryption (SQLCipher). Too literal? |
| **Consecration** | Dedication to a sacred purpose | Medium | The act of encrypting = consecrating the data. |

**Top picks**: **Covenant** (E2E encryption layer), **Sanctum** (encrypted storage), **Seal** (message signing)
